ZIOS: End-to-end unified model for zero-shot speech generation with pitch control

Yoon-Cheol Ju, Il-Hwan Kim, Jihwan Park, Hyung Yong Kim, Hong-Sun Yang, Shukjae Choi, Shinji Watanabe

ABSTRACT

Recent studies show that deep neural networks are capable of zero-shot speech generation in which the model synthesizes the speech using the speaker identity extracted from the given audio. However, since the additional functionalities of prior research such as prosody control have not been fully integrated to zero-shot speech generation, it shortens the practicality of the model. Moreover, it has not been extensively researched to disentangle the speaker identity into the number of basis vectors that are controllable to either generate or modify the speaker identity. We propose ZIOS, a zero-shot speech generation model that enables the control of the prosody elements of the generated speech such as pitch. Moreover, the proposed speaker quantization module establishes the representational capability of the speaker vector space by utilizing a fixed number of basis vectors, which facilitates the random generation or modification of the speaker identity. In total, the model is able to perform four applications, i.e., zero-shot text-to-speech and voice conversion, random speaker generation, and prosody control, under an end-to-end unified framework with single-step joint training. The experiment results show that it outperforms the other state-of-the-art models with high speech naturalness and speaker similarity, and the pitch element is controllable with the model in zero-shot speech generation. Furthermore, we ensure that the speaker identities of the generated speeches using random speaker generation do not overlap with those of the training sets.
